Motivation

How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

I really enjoyed reading this chapter. Perhaps a 'aha' moment in some respects. I think I fall into the same trap as many, dedicating too much time to pleasure and letting my ego take over...impulsive in some ways. I have been fortunate to coach tennis as my daily job for the last 12 years, which is a huge passion of mine and a great source of enjoyment. This service I've been providing can also be a 'higher purpose' in some ways. I've been teaching children and adults; self-discpline, good physical habits, to control their emotions and think tactically under pressure. I've got to the point where I want a complete change though. No doubt I will be back coaching on a part time basis sometime in the future. Back to the question, what does life look like? Well when we constantly chase pleasure we are only ever happy for a short time, we crave that next hit of pleasure and quite often its never enough. When we pursue life through a passion we can maintain our focus and enjoyment for much longer, but evenually we can become bord. Life through the 'higher purpose' lens is selfless, but fulfilling. We are focused on achieving something bigger than ourselves, something for others and we find that our ego is alleviated and can maintain and enjoy consistent happiness...Let's find out :)
